White Hall

This logo was a project aiming at revitalizing an Alabama town by giving it a new logo. There was a focus on form and meaning behind the logo mark. Research into the town of White Hall led to small details that played an important part in the final design of the logo. The mark has a river running through a green spherical shape. This showed the bliss of the rural town while contrasting with the strong serifs in the display font. The font was chosen because it embodied the name ‘White Hall’ and the power that could be seen in the architecture of the town hall.

With an identity established I went through several iterations before landing on a finished logo. The final mark began as an abstract use of the letter S in four directions. The logo consists of 4 curved teardrop shaped abstract marks. The abstract mark can be seen as small ghosts or a camera shutter utilizing the circular whitespace in the marks center. I chose Century Gothic for the type because the equality and symmetry between the forms gave a solid sophisticated form.
